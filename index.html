Research

I'm interested in vision-language models, reinforcement learning, and continual learning. My research focuses on building intelligent systems that can understand visual and textual information jointly, adapt to new tasks over time, and transfer knowledge efficiently across domains. Some of my papers are highlighted.

CKA-RL: Continual Knowledge Adaptation for Reinforcement Learning
Jinwu Hu*, Zihao Lian* (co-first author), Zhiquan Wen, Chenghao Li, Guohao Chen, Xutao Wen, Bin Xiao, Mingkui Tan
NeurIPS, 2025
code / paper

We propose CKA-RL, a Continual Reinforcement Learning framework that enables efficient knowledge accumulation and transfer across tasks through a knowledge vector pool and an adaptive knowledge merging mechanism. CKA-RL alleviates catastrophic forgetting and improves forward transfer by 4.20%, demonstrating strong adaptability in non-stationary environments.

NSG-VD: Physics-driven AI-generated Video Detection via Normalized Spatiotemporal Gradient
Shuhai Zhang*, Zihao Lian* (co-first author), Jiahao Yang, Daiyuan Li, Guoxuan Pang, Feng Liu, Bo Han, Shutao Li, Mingkui Tan
NeurIPS, 2025
code / paper

We propose NSG-VD, a physics-driven method for detecting AI-generated videos based on probability flow conservation. By introducing the Normalized Spatiotemporal Gradient (NSG), our approach quantifies deviations from natural motion dynamics and achieves state-of-the-art performance, surpassing baselines by 16.00% in Recall and 10.75% in F1-Score.
